### **1. What is DSBDA?**

**DSBDA** stands for **Data Science and Big Data Analytics**.
It is often a subject/course in Computer Engineering or IT that teaches students how to:

* Analyze large datasets
* Apply machine learning
* Use tools like Python, R, Hadoop, Spark, etc.
* Solve real-world problems using data-driven techniques

---

### **2. What is Data Science?**

**Data Science** is the field that combines statistics, computer science, and domain knowledge to:

* Collect, clean, and analyze data
* Extract insights and knowledge
* Support decision-making using data
  Key components include:
* Data Analysis
* Machine Learning
* Data Visualization
* Predictive Modeling

---

### **3. What is Big Data?**

**Big Data** refers to extremely large and complex datasets that traditional tools can't process efficiently.
It is defined by the **5 Vs**:

1. **Volume** – Huge amount of data
2. **Velocity** – Speed at which data is generated
3. **Variety** – Different data formats (text, image, video, etc.)
4. **Veracity** – Uncertainty or inconsistency in data
5. **Value** – Extracting useful information

Technologies used: **Hadoop**, **Spark**, **NoSQL databases**, etc.

---

### **4. Applications of Data Science**

* **Healthcare** – Disease prediction, medical image analysis
* **Finance** – Fraud detection, credit scoring
* **Retail** – Customer segmentation, recommendation systems
* **Manufacturing** – Predictive maintenance, process optimization
* **Sports** – Performance analysis, injury prediction
* **Marketing** – Customer behavior prediction, sentiment analysis

---

### **5. Applications of Big Data**

* **Social Media Analysis** – Sentiment and trend analysis
* **Smart Cities** – Traffic, pollution, and energy management
* **E-commerce** – Personalized recommendations, supply chain analytics
* **Banking** – Real-time risk analysis, customer profiling
* **Telecom** – Network optimization, churn prediction
* **IoT** – Sensor data analytics, automation

---

### **Advantages and Disadvantages**

#### **Data Science**

**Advantages:**

* Enables data-driven decisions
* Improves product and service quality
* Helps identify trends and patterns
* Supports automation and AI applications

**Disadvantages:**

* Data privacy concerns
* Requires skilled professionals
* Biased or poor-quality data can lead to wrong conclusions
* High computational costs

---

#### **Big Data**

**Advantages:**

* Processes huge volumes of data quickly
* Supports real-time analytics
* Helps in forecasting and trend analysis
* Drives innovation in multiple sectors

**Disadvantages:**

* Requires large storage and computing infrastructure
* Complex to manage and secure
* Scalability and data integration challenges
* Data quality and consistency issues
